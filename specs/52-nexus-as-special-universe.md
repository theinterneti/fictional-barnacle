## 1. Purpose

The Nexus is TTA's inter-universe hub: a place between stories where actors
from different universes can coexist. S52 defines the Nexus's mechanical
structure — what kind of universe it is, how its composition differs from
standard universes, who its inhabitants are, and what default content it
contains.

S53 defines *how and why players reach the Nexus* (narrative triggers, gating).
S52 defines *what the Nexus is* at the engine level.

**Core design principle**: The Nexus is *not* a special engine concept. It is
a universe whose S39 composition declares a distinctive set of properties that
allow cross-universe inhabitants. No new engine primitives are introduced.

---

## 2. Nexus Composition

The Nexus universe manifest (a YAML file in `data/universes/nexus/manifest.yaml`)
uses the S39 composition schema with the following distinctive settings:

```yaml
universe_id: nexus
display_name: "The Nexus"
composition_type: nexus

# Nexus-specific composition flags
allow_cross_universe_actors: true   # actors from any universe may be present
reality_coherence: low              # rules of physics are loose; contradiction is tolerated
genre_blend: true                   # all genre vocabularies co-exist

# Thematic foundation
primary_theme: threshold            # liminal space; between worlds
secondary_themes: [memory, reflection, possibility]

# Standard S39 fields
seed: nexus-seed-001
version: 1
```

### 2.1 What `allow_cross_universe_actors` Enables

When `allow_cross_universe_actors: true`, the universe accepts actors whose
`source_universe_id` differs from the Nexus's own `universe_id`. In a standard
universe, actors with a foreign `source_universe_id` are rejected. In the Nexus,
they are valid and their echo memories are surfaced without the usual halving
of importance (because the Nexus *exists to receive travelers*).

## 3. Nexus Inhabitants (NPCs)

The Nexus ships with three built-in NPC archetypes:

| Archetype | Role | Narrative function |
|---|---|---|
| **Wayfinder** | Neutral guide | Aware of the multiverse; helps travelers orient; knows rough lore about other universes |
| **The Archivist** | Keeper of stories | Holds echoes of events from all universes that have passed through; can surface half-remembered stories |
| **The Liminal** | Enigmatic resident | Has lived in the Nexus so long they have lost their source universe; represents what happens if you stay |

These archetypes are defined as NPC templates in the Nexus manifest. Individual
NPC instances are generated by the LLM at session start, seeded from the template.

---

## 4. Default Locations

The Nexus manifest defines three default locations:

| Location ID | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `nexus:crossroads` | The Crossroads | Entry point for all travelers; where arrivals manifest |
| `nexus:archive` | The Archive | A vast, shifting library of remembered stories; Archivist's domain |
| `nexus:edge` | The Edge | Where the Nexus frays into nothing; a place for reflection or departure |

Actors arriving via S51 always spawn at `nexus:crossroads`.

## 5. Functional Requirements

### FR-52.01 — Nexus Is a Universe Instance

The Nexus MUST be loaded, managed, and evicted through the same `UniverseRegistry`
as all other universes (S50). No engine special-casing. The Nexus's `universe_id`
is the string literal `"nexus"`.

### FR-52.02 — Nexus Is Always Resident

The Nexus universe MUST be counted as a pinned resource: it is never eligible
for LRU eviction as long as at least one actor is present OR the Nexus Access
Rules (S53) allow new arrivals. The `UniverseRegistry` tracks a `pinned: bool`
flag per universe.

### FR-52.03 — Cross-Universe Actor Acceptance

When an actor arrives in the Nexus from Universe X, their echo memories are
surfaced at full importance (no halving). The arrival onboarding (S51 FR-51.03)
is adapted with a Nexus-specific crossing narration prompt that emphasizes the
liminal, between-worlds nature of the space.

### FR-52.04 — Actor Coexistence

Multiple actors from different source universes MAY occupy the Nexus
simultaneously. They share the Nexus world-state (same locations, same NPC
instances). Turn processing for each actor is independent (no S58 conflict
logic required for the Nexus unless S57 multiplayer is also active).

### FR-52.05 — Reality Coherence = Low

The Nexus's `reality_coherence: low` setting instructs the LLM turn pipeline
to apply relaxed contradiction-detection (S06 narrative coherence check is
downweighted). The Nexus is explicitly a place where the impossible is
mundane; strict world-model consistency is not enforced.

## 6. Acceptance Criteria (Gherkin)

```gherkin
Feature: Nexus as Special Universe

  Scenario: AC-52.01 — Nexus loads via standard UniverseRegistry
    Given the UniverseRegistry receives a load("nexus") call
    When the Nexus universe loads
    Then it reaches ACTIVE state through the standard lifecycle (S50)
    And no special engine code path is executed

  Scenario: AC-52.02 — Nexus accepts actors from any source universe
    Given actor A from Universe X and actor B from Universe Y
    When both travel to the Nexus (S51)
    Then both actors exist in the Nexus with their respective source_universe_id
    And both can take turns in the Nexus world

  Scenario: AC-52.03 — Echo memories are at full importance in the Nexus
    Given actor A has a cross_universe MemoryRecord with importance_score = 0.8
    And actor A travels from Universe X to the Nexus
    When the Nexus turn pipeline injects memories
    Then the MemoryRecord importance_score is 0.8 (not 0.4)

  Scenario: AC-52.04 — Nexus is not evicted while actors are present
    Given the budget is exhausted and the Nexus has 1 resident actor
    When eviction is requested for the Nexus
    Then the eviction is refused
    And a different universe is evicted
```

## 7. Out of Scope

- Narrative triggers for reaching the Nexus (S53).
- Turn conflict resolution for multiple actors acting simultaneously (S58).
- The Nexus's long-term lore evolution (narrative content is LLM-generated).
